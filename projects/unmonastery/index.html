unMonastery

Coordinator, Researcher, and Technical Support

Previous Next

unMonastery is a way to radically re-vision distributed communities during a time of environmental, infrastructural, and economic volatility.

Interweaving the history of monasticism and hackerspace design patterns, the social innovation project is in the process of building a network of long-term and outward-facing cooperative living spaces to cultivate service-oriented, meaningful work as a means of both personal and collective resilience.

For nearly two years, Kei Kreutler has worked to establish unMonastery Test Lab co-living sites in Matera, Italy and Athens, Greece to support and work with the surrounding community, as well as contributed to the production a library of documentation, tools, templates and reflection on the present and possible futures of living together, known as the unMonastery BIOS.

Working toward establishing a long-term, larger scale residence in 2017, the unMonastery frames the possibility of an open source monastic order as a foundation for solidarity through adaptive, chimeric networks and sustainable mutual aid. The project welcomes participation.
